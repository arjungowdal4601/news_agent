## www_automotiveworld_com_sitemap

### Agentic-AI “scenario agents” for ADAS/AD—selective compute to fit existing vehicle hardware
- Architecture uses multiple specialised, scenario-focused agents rather than a single generalised model
- Only agents relevant to the current situation are activated, reducing compute load
- Claims enable advanced driving on standard sensor configurations and existing vehicle hardware
- Framed as addressing scaling limits of conventional end-to-end autonomy by changing how intelligence is organised
Source: https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/autobrains-deploys-agentic-ai-for-adas-and-ad-systems/

### Schaeffler proposes centralised SDV E/E with multi-domain controllers over Ethernet + RCP
- Centralised E/E architecture with multi-domain controllers to reduce decentralised complexity
- Uses Ethernet-based data channels and Remote Control Protocol for communications
- Claims hardware migration across vehicle generations and scalable deployment across vehicle classes
- Work spans vehicle control cluster (HV/LV electrical, sensors/actuators, drive systems, edge ECUs, zone controllers)
- Also references related development in 48V systems, power electronics, and safety-critical steer-by-wire redundancy
Source: https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/schaeffler-develops-centralised-e-e-architecture-for-sdvs/

### Google open-sources AAOS SDV—standardising non-safety software stack infrastructure for SDVs
- AAOS SDV defined as extension of Android Automotive OS covering non-safety SDV software stack
- Goal: common open infrastructure to reduce fragmentation across differing vehicle architectures
- Developed with partners including Renault Group and Qualcomm
- Release planned later in 2026
- Intended to extend beyond infotainment to broader vehicle software stack and support OTA updates
Source: https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/google-open-sources-aaos-sdv-platform-for-automakers/

### Holyvolt + Wildcat battery R&D stack: rapid electrode screen-printing + high-throughput combinatorial chemistry + data workflow
- Low-cost rapid screen-printing process for making anodes/cathodes; cathode uses proprietary water-based technology
- Claims reduced energy use, cost, and environmental footprint; “less complex” and faster than traditional processes
- High-throughput combinatorial chemistry running thousands of parallel experiments; claimed up to 10x faster than conventional methods
- Generates large cycling/battery data for computational/AI-driven materials improvement
- Developing DRX high-energy cathode described as no nickel/cobalt (pilot/demonstration scale; not producing batteries themselves)
Source: https://www.automotiveworld.com/articles/holyvolts-wildcat-acquisition-tackles-wests-ev-battery-lag/

### Volvo’s multi-adaptive adaptive belt: real-time sensor load setting + OTA updates
- Multi-adaptive safety belt adapts belt load settings using real-time sensor data
- Adjusts load based on crash severity and occupant profiles using interior and exterior sensor inputs
- Debuted on Volvo EX60 in 2026
- Over-the-air software updates for the safety system after launch
- Positioned as part of an integrated restraint suite alongside airbags, occupant detection, and driver-assistance systems
Source: https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/volvo-cars-wins-ajac-safety-award-for-adaptive-belt-tech/

### Fault-tolerant AV/UAV network coordination: NSF funds resilient decision-making to prevent cascading failures
- NSF grant (US$534,264) for RESONET: Resilient and Secure Operation of Networked Real-Time Systems
- Develops a fault-tolerant coordination framework for autonomous vehicles and UAVs
- Treats decision-making within/between systems as a continuous process with mechanisms to avoid single-point failures
- Aims to prevent cascading failures across a fleet
- Includes prototyping, proof-of-concept testing, and hardware-in-the-loop validation
Source: https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/kentucky-researcher-wins-nsf-grant-for-av-safety-framework/

### Einride Level 4 trucks: public safety self-assessment with audited safety case + redundancy + deterministic checker
- Einride publishes a Voluntary Safety Self-Assessment (VSSA) for SAE Level 4 cab-less autonomous heavy-duty trucks
- Safety case described as third-party audited and aligned to UL 4600, ISO 26262, and ISO/PAS 21448
- Covers operational design domain, fallback strategies, and lifecycle safety obligations
- Technical resilience: redundancy across steering, braking, power, sensing, and compute
- Driving approach: machine learning-based driving plus an independent deterministic safety checker
Source: https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/einride-publishes-safety-self-assessment-for-level-4-trucks/

### Toyota circular manufacturing: closed-loop aluminium from end-of-life wheels into Corolla engine components
- Toyota Circular Factory (TCF) at TMUK Burnaston recovers aluminium from end-of-life alloy wheels
- Recovered aluminium is recast and used to make engine components for the Toyota Corolla
- Material flow: TCF-UK transports aluminium to Toyota Deeside for incorporation into hybrid power unit production, then returns to Burnaston for Corolla assembly
- Processes vehicles from other brands to generate dismantling/material-composition insights
- First vehicle using recovered material built on 19 March 2026; second TCF planned in Toyota Motor Manufacturing Poland (Wałbrzych)
Source: https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/tmuk-burnaston-closes-the-loop-on-end-of-life-aluminium/

### Amprius’ SiCore: silicon-anode EV cells move to scale in China
- SiCore uses a silicon anode claiming up to 100% higher energy density than conventional graphite anodes
- Rated cycle life stated as >2,000 cycles
- Cylindrical cell format: 30 Ah capacity cells
- US$21m purchase order for SiCore cells covering light EV applications (scooters/3-wheelers/motorcycles)
Source: https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/amprius-lands-us21m-sicore-battery-deal-in-china/

### Valeo breaks ground on Texas SDV central compute unit manufacturing for GM
- US$225m McAllen, Texas plant; 337,000 sq ft footprint
- Investment over five years; production planned for late 2027
- Focus: central compute units for GM’s next-generation electrical architecture
- Compute unit described as liquid-cooled system processing data from multiple sensors/systems for essential vehicle functions
Source: https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/valeo-breaks-ground-on-us225m-texas-sdv-plant/

### AutoLOOP adds traction-battery recycling: traceability and closed-loop material recovery
- Expanded AutoLOOP digital platform to cover EV traction battery collection and recycling streams
- Records battery ID, vehicle ID, and battery status/condition before directing to specialized recycling sites
- Closed-loop model for recovery of strategic metals (lithium/cobalt/nickel) for reuse in new batteries
- Supports EU extended producer responsibility (EPR) compliance from Aug 2025 with manufacturer financing and traceability obligations
Source: https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/the-future-is-neutral-adds-ev-battery-recycling-to-autoloop/
